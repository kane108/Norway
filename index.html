Tours

Oslo

Oslo is the capital of Norway, a country in northern Europe. It is Norway’s largest city and busiest seaport. Most of the country’s major museums, theaters, and other cultural centers are in Oslo. It is also famous for the Vigeland Sculpture Park, Holmenkollen ski jumping hill, and Munch. It is also the home of the group that awards the Nobel peace prize.

Bergen

This city was so important in Medieval times, it was the Norwegian capital for two centuries. These days, it is the country's second-largest city filled with cultural attractions and beautiful scenery. Bryggen Hanseatic Wharf (Tyskebryggen), for instance, is a lovely mix of food and culture owing to the district's restaurants and old-school workshops. The Hanseatic Museum and Schøtstuene, meanwhile, showcase 18th-century weapons and other artifacts. For lovers of the quirky artwork of Edvard Munch, KODE3 is their best bet. Travelers who want to enjoy a panoramic view of the city can walk or ride a funicular up Mount Fløyen.

Tromsø

Tromso is a city in Norway where visitors explore polar fishing excursions, whale safaris, and wildlife safaris. Arctic Cathedral in Tromso is an iconic landmark that hosts concerts throughout the year, but the Midnight Sun Concerts during summers are the highlight. Another one here is the magic of the Northern Lights that give glimpses of the shimmer of the dancing aurora. Aurora dinner cruise is another spectacular experience. During winter, the husky sled ride is the most exciting sled ride to explore Tromso. Ride on the Fjellheisen cable car on the Storsteinen mountains is breathtaking.

Trondheim

Trondheim, Norway, is the technology capital of Norway, where guests can visit Norway's Science and Technology University. Aside from this, the city is also home to the beautiful Nidaros Cathedral. It is a medieval church and palace that now features informative exhibits. Guests will love exploring it as it also features a scenic atmosphere
